Mastering the Scotched Suit

By The Editorial Team | December 6, 2025

A scotched (or plaid) suit is often seen as a risky move. It’s bold, it’s loud, and if done wrong, it can look chaotic. But done right? It is the ultimate power move.

1. The Rule of Solids

When your suit has a pattern, everything else must be quiet. Pair your Grey Windowpane Check with a solid white or light blue shirt. Avoid patterned ties; a solid slate or charcoal knitted tie adds texture without clashing.

2. The Fit Matters More

Because the pattern draws the eye, the fit must be impeccable. A baggy scotched suit looks messy. Ensure the shoulders are sharp and the trousers have a clean break.

"Fashion fades, style is eternal. The scotched suit is for the man who knows the difference."

3. Footwear

Stick to classics. Black oxfords for the city, dark brown brogues for a slightly more relaxed look. Never wear sneakers with a double-breasted scotched suit.
